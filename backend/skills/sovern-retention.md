# Sovern House — Buyer Retention & Expansion

**Version:** 1.0 | **Created:** 2026-04-18
**Depends on:** `sovern-icp.md`, `sovern-email-writer.md`, `sovern-gmail-outreach.md`, `trade-customer.md`, `trade-sales.md`, `sovern-egypt-lane.md` (for Egypt-specific context)
**Use for:** Post-first-deal buyer retention, reorder cultivation, cross-sell to new product categories, and growing individual buyer relationships from transactional to strategic.

---

## Why this skill exists

Trading-house economics are retention-driven. Acquiring a new buyer costs 5-10× more than expanding an existing relationship. A single buyer who moves from one container per quarter to one per month represents 3-4× the revenue impact of a same-sized new buyer — and comes at a fraction of the acquisition cost.

Most outreach and sales energy goes into the first deal. This skill focuses on the 10 deals AFTER the first.

## The buyer lifecycle

Every Sovern House buyer passes through these stages. Each has a different operating cadence and goal.

### Stage 1 — First Contact
- Initial RFQ or inbound inquiry
- Qualification per `sovern-icp.md`
- First email response within 4 hours (per trust architecture)
- Owned by: Alex (or the appropriate Country Manager for regional leads)

### Stage 2 — First Deal
- Quote issued, negotiated, contracted
- First container sourced, inspected, shipped
- First invoice paid
- Goal: on-time, on-spec, on-budget. Zero surprises. This deal determines whether there's a Stage 3.
- Owned by: Alex primarily, supported by operations

### Stage 3 — Repeat Business (post-first-deal)
- Second, third, fourth orders in the same category
- Goal: make reordering effortless. Buyer should never "shop around" because we've made reordering the easy path.
- Retention tactics:
  - **Check-in call 2 weeks after container delivery** — "How did the shipment perform? Any issues in the warehouse / workshop / retail?"
  - **Quality-assured reorder workflow** — if original spec is clean, reorder is a simple email confirming quantity + timing, not a new RFQ
  - **Proactive stock-alert** — if we know typical reorder cadence, flag buyer a week before they'd normally reorder: "You typically reorder ~6 weeks after delivery. Current factory lead time is 45 days. Shall we initiate?"
  - **Price stability** — where margins allow, hold price for 90 days after a successful first order
- Owned by: Alex + dedicated account manager concept

### Stage 4 — Category Expansion
- Buyer expands from one product category to a second or third
- Goal: leverage the trust earned to unlock adjacent categories
- Expansion tactics:
  - **Natural adjacencies** — flooring buyer → bathroom/hardware, auto parts buyer → tools + fasteners, garment buyer → home textiles
  - **"What else do you import?" conversation** — asked at the 3-month or 6-month relationship mark, not day 1
  - **Bundle incentives** — favorable terms on a new category trial order when paired with an existing reorder
  - **Supplier introductions** — leverage the trust to introduce the buyer to a new factory for a new category
- Owned by: Alex + account manager

### Stage 5 — Strategic Partnership
- Buyer's volume, relationship depth, and tenure qualify them as a strategic partner (top 10% of revenue typically)
- Goal: transition from "buyer-supplier" relationship to "co-invested partner"
- Strategic tactics:
  - **Joint factory visits** — invite the buyer to visit factories with you
  - **Exclusive sourcing** — offer exclusive access to certain factory capacity or product variations
  - **Private label / OEM arrangements** — Sovern manages the supply of buyer-branded products
  - **Year-ahead planning** — instead of order-by-order, plan annual volumes and reserve capacity
  - **Contract formalization** — move from PO-by-PO to framework agreement with volume commitments
- Owned by: Alex directly

### Stage 6 — Dormant / Churn Risk
- Buyer stops ordering for 4-6 months after a repeat history
- Goal: identify why, and either reactivate or cleanly close the relationship
- Reactivation tactics:
  - **Direct phone call or in-person meeting** (not email) — "We noticed no orders for 6 months. What changed?"
  - **Honest diagnosis** — their business changed? Our service slipped? A competitor entered? An internal champion left?
  - **Specific reactivation offer** — tied to the diagnosed reason (new product category, adjusted terms, staff change on our side)
  - **If truly dormant** — leave on good terms; don't burn the relationship with aggressive win-back
- Owned by: Alex

## Retention touchpoints — what to do when

### During the first deal (Stage 2)
- Day 0 (contract signed): confirmation email with full timeline + contact protocol
- Day 14 (production started): progress update with factory photos if possible
- Day 30 (QC scheduled): QC plan shared
- Day 45 (pre-shipment): inspection report + shipping docs
- Day X (container arrives at port): arrival notification + clearance support
- Day X+7 (container received at buyer's warehouse): delivery confirmation + feedback request

### Post-delivery (Stage 3)
- Day +14 post-delivery: quality feedback call or email
- Day +30: thank-you note; if feedback was positive, a simple "ready when you are for the next one"
- Quarterly: relationship check-in (not a sales call; just "how's things?")
- Annually: market update — what's trending in factories we partner with, what buyers in their segment are ordering

### Around the order cycle (Stages 3-4)
- 6 weeks before typical reorder: proactive "shall we initiate?" message
- 2 weeks before reorder deadline: confirmation of lead time + capacity
- Throughout: available via WhatsApp / phone / email on ~same-day response

### Strategic cadence (Stage 5)
- Quarterly strategic review
- Annual planning session
- Factory visit invitations (once per year minimum)
- Year-end relationship summary — volumes, performance, outlook

## Category-specific retention dynamics

### Flooring
- **Reorder cycle:** typically quarterly to semi-annually for retailers, project-based for contractors
- **Upsell path:** hardwood → engineered → laminate; flooring → baseboards/trim → accessories
- **Retention risk:** local competition offering marginally cheaper; defend with QC track record + relationship

### Garments & Fabrics
- **Reorder cycle:** seasonal (fashion), quarterly (home textiles), monthly (technical fabrics)
- **Upsell path:** core garment → accessories, home textiles; technical fabrics → related industrial textiles
- **Retention risk:** fashion cycles make category migration natural; ride with the buyer

### Bathroom & Hardware
- **Reorder cycle:** project-based (new builds, renovations); quarterly for distributors
- **Upsell path:** bathroom fittings → sanitary ware → architectural hardware → specialty/commercial
- **Retention risk:** certification expiration (CE, WATERMARK) can gate reorders; stay ahead

### Auto Accessories & Parts
- **Reorder cycle:** monthly to quarterly for high-frequency items (filters, fluids, consumables); semi-annual for interior accessories
- **Upsell path:** consumables → interior accessories → mechanical parts → lighting/electronics (per `sovern-egypt-lane.md` tier progression, internal strategy)
- **Retention risk:** Chinese brand loyalty is fragile; deliver consistently or get replaced by the next competitor offer

## Red flags — buyer relationship in trouble

Watch for these in ongoing buyer relationships:

- **Response time lengthening** — they used to reply in 24 hours, now taking 72 hours
- **Order sizes shrinking** — half the volume of previous orders for no disclosed reason
- **Payment delays** — pattern of late payments despite documented terms
- **Communication narrowing** — they used to ask about new products / market updates; now just transactional
- **Staff churn on their side** — a champion buyer leaves the buyer company; new buyer doesn't have the same relationship equity
- **Competitor mention** — they casually mention "I got a quote from [another trading house]"
- **Complaints that don't resolve** — the same complaint surfaces twice; suggests systemic issue not just one-off

When 2+ of these signals appear, trigger a Stage 6 diagnostic conversation.

## Growth metrics to track per buyer

For any buyer past Stage 2, track:

- **Time since first order** (tenure)
- **Total order count** (frequency)
- **Total volume** (containers / units / dollars)
- **Average order interval** (inferred reorder cadence)
- **Revenue trend** (last 4 orders vs. prior 4 orders — growing, stable, declining)
- **Category count** (1 = focused buyer; 3+ = expanded buyer)
- **Payment history** (on-time %, average DSO)
- **Relationship depth** — qualitative: how many people on each side are in communication? Just Alex ↔ buyer's procurement? Or multiple touchpoints?

Build a simple buyer scorecard: a Google Sheet or Airtable with one row per buyer, columns for these metrics, updated quarterly. Score buyers:

- **Green** — growing, multi-category, strategic partnership candidates
- **Yellow** — stable, single-category, check-in regularly
- **Red** — declining, churn risk, diagnostic conversation needed

## Egyptian buyer retention specifics

See `sovern-egypt-lane.md` for full Egypt context. Retention nuances for Egyptian buyers:

- **Relationship intensity is higher** — Egyptian B2B buyers expect more frequent relationship touchpoints than Western buyers
- **Ramadan cadence** — no major sales or upsell push during Ramadan; instead, relationship-warming messages (Eid greetings, check-ins without sales agenda)
- **Payment flexibility as retention** — Egyptian buyers facing CBE forex constraints appreciate payment term flexibility; Sovern offering terms competitors won't = sticky retention
- **Country Manager touchpoints** — your Country Manager — Egypt handles day-to-day relationship management; Alex handles quarterly strategic / annual planning

1. **2 weeks after any first-deal delivery** — run the post-delivery cadence
2. **When a buyer's order interval starts to lengthen** — diagnose per Stage 6 framework
3. **Quarterly** — review the buyer scorecard; trigger check-ins on yellow-scored buyers
4. **When planning category expansion** — map against Stage 4 adjacency logic
5. **Before major relationship moments** — annual planning, new category launches, Ramadan, end-of-year

## Why this skill exists

LinkedIn is the highest-ROI channel for B2B trade — Western importers, corporate procurement teams, institutional buyers, and VCs all evaluate founders through LinkedIn before meetings. A poorly positioned personal profile or missing Company Page leaks trust. A well-structured LinkedIn presence compounds authority over months, creating inbound lead flow that cold outbound can't match.

Per 2026 research:
- **Personal profiles generate 561% more reach** than company pages on LinkedIn
- **Algorithm rewards single-niche consistency** — hybrid founders (multi-venture) must either pick one focus OR find a coherent umbrella thesis
- **Headline formula: Role + Niche + Outcome + Authority** — 220 characters, appears everywhere
- **About section: first 2-3 lines are the hook** — "see more" cuts off everything else; lead with audience pain, not resume

## Sovern House personal LinkedIn — positioning framework

### The strategic constraint

Alex operates two ventures with thematically incompatible audiences:
- **Sovern House** — institutional B2B trade (Western importers, procurement teams, Egyptian wholesalers)
- **Vendetta Games** — Web3 crypto gaming (crypto-native players + VCs for the upcoming equity sale)

Rather than force algorithmic coherence onto incoherent ventures, Sovern House's LinkedIn strategy accepts the multi-venture reality and uses a **unifying founder thesis** — then weights the profile toward Sovern for discoverability by B2B importer buyers, while preserving Vendetta visibility for VC discoverability.

### The unifying thesis

> **"Businesses that put sovereign ownership in the hands of the people who use them."**

- **Sovern House** → buyers gain sovereign control over their Asia supply chain. No intermediary markups, no agency opacity. Direct-to-factory sourcing with transparent pricing.
- **Vendetta Games** → players gain sovereign ownership of their game assets onchain. No house-edge opacity, no centralized asset custody. Provably fair gaming with transparent economics.

Both ventures reject the same thing — opaque intermediation that extracts value from end users. The thesis is honest (not a post-hoc rationalization) and differentiates Alex from every other multi-venture founder who just lists ventures without a narrative.

### Headline formula — Sovern-primary with Vendetta embedded

Structure: **[Primary role + value] · [Audience outcome] · [Second venture for VC discoverability] · [Authority signal]**

Target: ≤ 220 characters (LinkedIn max).

## About section — the Sovern-primary thesis statement

Recommended structure (per 2026 research):

1. **Hook (first 2-3 lines, visible before "see more")** — lead with the audience's pain, not credentials
2. **Thesis** — what you believe about trade sourcing that most buying houses don't
3. **Proof** — specific verifiable facts (30 years, languages, geographies)
4. **Second venture acknowledgment** — short paragraph acknowledging Vendetta for VC/investor visibility, framed through the unifying thesis
5. **Clear next step** — specific CTA (book a call, reach out for a buying office consultation, etc.)

Avoid:
- Resume bullet points ("Over 30 years of experience in...")
- Clichés ("passionate about," "results-driven," "innovative")
- Third-person voice ("Alexander has built...")
- Selling Vendetta on a Sovern-leaning profile (VCs want to find it; Sovern buyers shouldn't be sold into crypto gaming)

LinkedIn allows 4 featured items at the top of the profile. Each slot earns its place.

**Recommended slots:**

1. **Sovern House website link** with compelling preview — drives qualified traffic

4. **Vendetta Games site or pitch deck link** — for VCs. Doesn't need prominence but needs discoverability.

## Content pillars — what to post, how often

For B2B trade LinkedIn, quality > frequency. Target: 2-3 posts/week minimum, 4-5 at peak.

### Pillar 1 — Factory & sourcing insight (40%)
Ground-truth observations from real factory visits, supplier meetings, inspections. Specific, photographed where possible, non-identifying.
- "Visited a flooring factory in Guangdong this week. Three observations about the current hardwood market..."
- "Why Chinese sun-shade factories are winning against Thai competitors — a quality breakdown"

### Pillar 2 — Trade mechanics and regulation (20%)
Explainers on topics most Western importers don't know well — Incoterms, customs clearance, country-specific import rules.
- "The 5 things Western importers keep getting wrong about Egyptian customs"
- "Why your CIF quote isn't the cost you'll actually pay"

### Pillar 3 — Founder thinking / thesis (15%)
The "sovereign ownership" thesis and its application. Positions Alex as a thinker, not just an operator.
- "Why I think buying houses will replace brokers over the next decade"
- "Transparency is infrastructure, not marketing"

### Pillar 4 — Vendetta / Web3 gaming (10%)
Small share — enough for VCs to find context, not enough to muddy the Sovern positioning. Frame through the unifying thesis when possible.
- "Lessons from running a provably fair onchain casino"
- "Why sovereign ownership matters in gaming — and why it's harder than it looks"

### Pillar 5 — Client wins & trade stories (15%)
Anonymized case studies, container arrivals, factory partnerships that landed, problems solved.
- "How we fixed a 40ft container of mislabeled garments before it left port"
- "90 days from first inquiry to landed flooring in a Vancouver retailer's warehouse"

## Engagement strategy

Posting alone doesn't grow; engagement compounds reach.

### Daily micro-actions (15-30 min)
- Comment substantively on 3-5 posts in your target audience's feed (other trade founders, Western import professionals, shipping/freight thought leaders, relevant VCs for Vendetta)
- Respond to every comment on your own posts within 24 hours
- Check DMs daily, respond within same business day (per trust-architecture responsiveness pillar)

### Weekly actions (60-90 min)
- Send 8-12 personalized connection requests per `outbound-day-1-playbook.md`
- Publish 2-3 substantive posts per week
- Write 1 longer-form post (600+ words) or article per week

### What NOT to do
- Don't use LinkedIn auto-posters or automation tools (algorithm penalizes)
- Don't post engagement-bait ("Agree? 👇")
- Don't comment with generic "Great post!" — adds no signal
- Don't mass-connect with generic requests ("I'd like to connect")
- Don't cross-post Vendetta crypto content heavily on LinkedIn — the audiences don't overlap enough to justify the brand noise

## How to invoke this skill

1. **Before any LinkedIn profile change** — audit against the headline formula, About structure, and Featured content plan
2. **When drafting a LinkedIn post** — map to one of the five content pillars
3. **When engaging a VC or institutional buyer through LinkedIn** — check positioning coherence (is what they see when they look at your profile consistent with the conversation you're about to have?)
4. **Quarterly** — re-audit the profile; platform evolves, best practices shift
5. **When content drifts off-thesis** — stop, re-center on the unifying thesis, re-draft

# Sovern House — Ideal Client Profile (ICP) Skill

**Version:** 1.0 | **Last Updated:** April 2026  
**Read before:** Any prospect sourcing, email writing, or campaign targeting task.

\---

## What This Skill Does

Defines the exact profile of a prospect Sovern House should spend time on. Every email, every sourcing session, every follow-up sequence must be validated against this ICP before execution. If a prospect doesn't fit, don't pursue — time is the scarcest resource.

\---

## The Core Buyer Problem Sovern House Solves

International buyers sourcing from Asia have ONE core fear: **being burned.**  
Wrong specs. Bad batches. Factories that pass inspection and ship differently. Language barriers that hide problems. Middlemen with hidden markups. Alex's 30-year ground presence, Mandarin/Taiwanese fluency, and QC expertise directly addresses every one of these fears. Every outreach message must connect to one of these pain points.

\---

## ICP Tier 1 — Primary Target (Highest Value, Best Fit)

### Who They Are

* **Company type:** Importers, distributors, wholesalers, retailers with private label programs, contractors/developers (for building products)
* **Geography:** North America (US primary, Canada), Europe (EU + UK), Latin America (secondary)
* **Company size:** $2M–$200M revenue; 5–500 employees. Large enough to have real sourcing needs, small enough to lack a physical Asia office.
* **Decision maker titles to target:**

  * Procurement Manager / Director of Procurement
  * Sourcing Manager / Head of Sourcing
  * VP of Supply Chain / Operations
  * Owner / Founder / CEO (for companies under \~30 people)
  * Import Manager
  * Product Development Manager (for private label)

### What They're Currently Doing (and hating)

* Sourcing via Alibaba or Global Sources with no on-the-ground verification
* Using an agent they don't fully trust (hidden markups, language gaps)
* Managing quality issues after goods arrive (too late)
* Visiting Asia 1-2x per year and guessing in between
* Paying a 10–15% middleman fee with zero transparency

### Their Ideal Outcome (What to Promise)

* A trusted extension of their office in Asia — someone who is there so they don't have to be
* Factory price transparency with a flat, disclosed commission (5% FOB)
* QC problems caught before goods leave origin
* Mandarin/Taiwanese negotiation that gets them the real price, not the "Western buyer" price
* Full documentation handled — they receive compliant, trade-ready shipments

### Category-Specific Sub-Profiles

**Flooring buyers (highest priority):**  
Distributors, flooring retailers, contractors, builders, property developers. Pain: certifications (formaldehyde, fire rating, slip resistance) are hard to verify from a distance. Sovern House's QC expertise is the pitch.

**Garments/fabrics buyers:**  
Apparel brands, retailers, private label buyers. Pain: fiber content compliance, country of origin labeling, restricted substances (REACH, CPSC). Language access to factory owners is critical here.

**Bathroom/ironmongery buyers:**  
Distributors, contractors, hospitality procurement. Pain: CE/ANSI certification compliance, technical spec accuracy. High-value per container — small error = big loss.

**Travel accessories buyers:**  
Retailers, e-commerce brands, corporate gifting companies. Lower unit values, higher volume. Good fit for private label programs.

## ICP Tier 2 — Secondary Target (Fit, Lower Priority)

* Companies actively diversifying OUT of China into Vietnam/Thailand suppliers (friend-shoring trend) 
* Other trading companies that need sourcing or QC support in categories where Sovern House has expertise
* Importers entering Asia for the first time — longer sales cycle but zero competitive barrier

\---

## Disqualified Prospects — Do Not Pursue

* **US government / public sector** (procurement complexity, compliance burden)
* **Consumer-direct / D2C brands** sourcing sample quantities (economics don't work at 5% commission on small orders)
* **Companies sourcing from non-Asia regions** (Latin America, Middle East, Africa) — not Sovern House's expertise
* **Competitors / other buying houses** — unless a subcontracting partnership is explicitly on the table
* **Companies with obvious sanctions exposure** — screen before ANY engagement (OFAC, EU list)

\---

## ICP Qualification Checklist (Run Before Outreach)

Before adding any prospect to an outreach campaign, verify:

* \[ ] They import or plan to import from Asia 
* \[ ] They operate in NA, EU, or LatAm
* \[ ] Company size suggests real sourcing volume (not one-time personal import)
* \[ ] Contact is a decision-maker or directly influences sourcing decisions
* \[ ] No obvious sanctions/compliance red flags (quick Google + OFAC name check)
* \[ ] They operate in at least one of Sovern House's core categories (or adjacent)
* \[ ] They would plausibly benefit from on-the-ground Asia representation

\---

## Intent Signals — Prioritize Prospects Showing These

High-priority outreach triggers:

* Job posting for "Asia Sourcing Manager" or "Import Specialist" → they're feeling the gap
* Recent LinkedIn posts about supply chain problems, quality issues, or China diversification
* Company recently expanded product line with new Asia-origin SKUs (per their website/catalog)
* Trade show attendance in their category (Canton Fair buyers, Heimtextil, Cersaie, etc.)
* Press mention of sourcing expansion or new import program

## Persona Voice Notes (How Each Buyer Talks)

**Procurement Manager at a mid-size distributor:**

> "We've been burned twice on quality. I need someone on the ground who will actually inspect, not just stamp a cert."

**Owner of a private label apparel brand:**

> "My agent just tells me what I want to hear. I never know if I'm getting the real factory price."

**Head of Supply Chain at a flooring retailer:**

> "We need CARB compliance on everything. The last batch cost us a fortune in returns."

Use this voice when writing email hooks. Mirror the language, not corporate pitch speak.
